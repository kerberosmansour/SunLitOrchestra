### Q2 — ICO enforcement reality for sub-seed companies (sharpened)

- **H1 2025 totals**: ICO took **15 enforcement actions**, a deliberate drop from 29 in H1 2024 — but total £ collected was **7× higher** than all of 2024; average fine jumped from ~£150,000 to **>£2.8M**. Two-thirds were UK GDPR breaches (vs one-sixth in 2024). High confidence. ([URM Consulting — Jan-Jun 2025 ICO analysis](https://www.urmconsulting.com/blog/analysis-of-ico-enforcement-action-january-june-2025), [Measured Collective](https://measuredcollective.com/ico-enforcement-in-2025-record-fines-and-what-they-mean/), [BDO ICO trends](https://www.bdo.co.uk/en-gb/insights/advisory/risk-and-advisory-services/trends-in-recent-ico-enforcement-action))
- **Article 13 transparency failures remain live**: audit themes (Aug 2023 – Jan 2024) flagged "fair processing notices that do not contain all the requirements of Article 13 of the UK GDPR" as a recurring finding. ICO state that every business — "however small" — needs a privacy notice when processing personal data. ([Mayer Brown](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2023/06/what-does-a-uk-gdpr-compliant-privacy-notice-look-like-lessons-learned-from-a-recent-ico-enforcement-decision), [ICO — Cookies & privacy notices in detail](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/advice-for-small-organisations/privacy-notices-and-cookies/cookies-and-privacy-notices-in-detail/))
- **Realistic sub-seed exposure** (inference from 2024/25 fine-shape): fines at £2M+ average mean ICO is **concentrating on large-impact breaches** (DNA Genetics £2.31M, large marketers). For a sub-£100k-turnover B2C startup, the **primary risk is reprimand + enforcement notice**, not fine — but the `draft` hard-block remains defensible because (a) monetary-penalty ceiling is £17.5M regardless of size, (b) ICO's published audits target privacy-notice deficiency as a category, and (c) the cost of a privacy notice done wrong is reputational + customer-trust, not only regulatory. **Confidence: medium-high**. The "never draft GDPR" posture is conservative but not over-conservative given the upside (one missed lawful-basis call can unwind an entire B2C launch).
- **Moving baseline**: Data (Use and Access) Act received Royal Assent **19 June 2025** and amends ICO enforcement powers. Any triage gate must treat the GDPR regime as a moving target, not a frozen 2018 baseline. ([Data Protection Report](https://www.dataprotectionreport.com/2025/07/uk-data-protection-reform-what-you-need-to-know-and-do/))

### Q3 — SEIS/EIS founder responsibility (sharpened to HMRC manual authority)

Top five voiding triggers `/slo-fundraise` triage must surface:

1. **Breach of control / independence requirement.** Company must not be a 51% subsidiary of another company, nor under control of another company or connected persons. Authority: **VCM34080**. ([GOV.UK VCM34080](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/venture-capital-schemes-manual/vcm34080))
2. **Disqualifying arrangements** under ITA07 s257HJ(1) — any scheme/agreement/understanding (enforceable or not) that would breach the independence requirement at any point during period A. ([GOV.UK VCM34080](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/venture-capital-schemes-manual/vcm34080))
3. **Preferential rights on share class** — ordinary shares granted preferential rights (via articles or SHA drafting, even without reissue) lose SEIS/EIS status. Case-law pattern: *Flix Innovations* line, new growth-share class disputes. ([RossMartin — EIS no relief for preferential rights](https://www.rossmartin.co.uk/sme-tax-news/4400-eis-no-relief-for-shares-with-preferential-rights), [Jonathan Lea Network — Common SEIS/EIS mistakes](https://www.jonathanlea.net/blog/common-seis-and-eis-mistakes/))
4. **Qualifying-trade drift into excluded activities** (VCM3000 series). Check early and at every pivot. ([GOV.UK VCM3000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/venture-capital-schemes-manual/vcm3000))
5. **Value extraction / non-independent transactions** to investors post-raise (SeedLegals' published list). ([SeedLegals 8 Things](https://seedlegals.com/resources/seis-eis-tax-relief-facts/))

Gate actions `/slo-fundraise` should force **before** any term-sheet drafting (inference from above):
- "Have you applied for Advance Assurance? (GOV.UK EIS apply guide)" — hard block.
- "Are you a subsidiary or controlled by another company (VCM34080)?" — hard block to accountant if yes.
- "Have you audited qualifying-trade status against VCM3000?" — warn.
- "Are any share rights preferential vs ordinary?" — hard block to solicitor if yes.

### Q4 — UK employment first-hire minimums + IR35/CEST (new evidence)

**Statutory minimums** (Employment Rights Act 1996, Working Time Regulations 1998, Pensions Act 2008):
- **Notice (ERA 1996 s86)**: employer gives 1 week if <2yrs service; 1 week per completed year up to a max of **12 weeks** (at 12+ yrs). Employee gives 1 week after 1 month of continuous service. Right triggers at 1-month qualifying service. ([legislation.gov.uk s86](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1996/18/section/86), [LexisNexis](https://www.lexisnexis.co.uk/legal/legislation/uk-parliament-acts/employment-rights-act-1996-c18/part-ix/section-86))
- **Holiday (WTR 1998)**: **5.6 weeks** paid leave per year = 28 days for full-time 5-day-week worker. From 2026, employers required to keep records adequate to show compliance. ([GOV.UK holiday reforms](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/simplifying-holiday-entitlement-and-holiday-pay-calculations/holiday-pay-and-entitlement-reforms-from-1-january-2024))
- **Pension auto-enrolment (2026/27)**: earnings trigger £10,000/yr, lower qualifying earnings £6,240, upper £50,270 — all held flat vs 2025/26. ([GOV.UK 2026/27 thresholds](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/review-of-the-automatic-enrolment-earnings-trigger-and-qualifying-earnings-band-for-202627/review-of-the-automatic-enrolment-earnings-trigger-and-qualifying-earnings-band-for-202627), [The Pensions Regulator](https://www.thepensionsregulator.gov.uk/en/employers/new-employers/im-an-employer-who-has-to-provide-a-pension/declare-your-compliance/ongoing-duties-for-employers/earnings-thresholds))

**IR35 / CEST decision factors** (for the contractor-vs-employee triage gate):
- Three primary indicators: **right of substitution**, **mutuality of obligation (MOO)**, **control** (how/when/where work is done). ([Contracteye IR35 CEST](https://www.contracteye.co.uk/ir35-what-is-hmrcs-cest-tool-what-are-its-limitations.shtml), [IT Contracting — MOO](https://www.itcontracting.com/ir35-status-what-is-the-mutuality-of-obligation-moo/))
- **Substitution** is the single strongest outside-IR35 indicator in HMRC case law.
- **CEST limitation**: the tool **does not weigh MOO** — HMRC's stance is that MOO exists whenever a contract is agreed, which is inconsistent with case law. Practically: a CEST "outside IR35" result is necessary but not sufficient. Any `/slo-legal` or `/slo-hire` triage that relies only on CEST must flag this. ([ir35guide.co.uk](https://ir35guide.co.uk/blog/hmrc-cest-tool-problems.html), [ContractorUK MOO 2026/27](https://www.contractoruk.com/ir35furtherreading/ir35-mutuality-obligation-202627-explainer-contractors))
- **Small-company thresholds raised 6 April 2026**: turnover £10.2M → **£15M**; balance sheet £5.1M → **£7.5M** (headcount 50 unchanged). Seed-stage clients remain "small" and therefore continue to rely on contractor self-assessment, not engager-side determination. ([GT Law, March 2026](https://www.gtlaw.com/en/insights/2026/3/threshold-changes-to-uk-off-payroll-working-rules-ir35-end-user-and-contractor-considerations))

**What hard-blocks to a lawyer + accountant** (inference): (a) substitution clause absent or sham; (b) contractor substantially full-time, exclusive, under direction; (c) CEST "employed" or "unable to determine" result; (d) engagement duration >6 months with rolling renewals; (e) contractor uses engager equipment + attends engager premises.

## Architecture Options

All three assume the repo's existing skill pattern: `skills/<name>/SKILL.md` + optional `references/` + optional `personas/` (precedent: `skills/slo-critique/{personas,references}`, `skills/slo-plan/references`).

### Option 1 — Shared `references/biz/` alongside four advisor skills (Approach B from the idea doc)

Structure:

```
skills/slo-legal/
  SKILL.md
  templates/{nda.md,contractor-sow.md,ip-assignment.md,terms-and-conditions.md}
skills/slo-accounting/SKILL.md
skills/slo-equity/SKILL.md
skills/slo-fundraise/SKILL.md
references/biz/
  triage-gate.md              # one source of truth for the four gates
  jurisdiction-uk.md          # UK-only prose/anchors; us.md/eu.md stubs
  cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md
  artifact-schema.md          # docs/biz/ frontmatter contract
  hmrc-vcm-index.md           # curated VCM anchor list + retrieval date
  ico-enforcement-index.md    # curated ICO guidance + DUAA moving-baseline note
  ir35-cest-factors.md        # three-factor + MOO limitation note
```

- **Trade-offs**: shared scaffolding prevents drift across four advisor skills (the failure mode the idea doc flagged). Matches existing repo convention (`skills/slo-critique/references`, `skills/slo-plan/references`) — new reviewer doesn't need to learn a new pattern.
- **Cost**: every advisor skill must *cite* the shared file (discipline, not enforcement). Prose duplication risk if a skill author inlines instead of linking.
- **Best fit**: the stated plan (four advisor skills, UK-first, ship `/slo-legal` in ≤ 1 week as Runbook A M1). **Primary recommendation.**

### Option 2 — Sibling `skills/_biz-shared/` as a non-executable shared skill

Structure:

```
skills/_biz-shared/
  SKILL.md             # a "library" skill with no trigger verbs
  triage-gate.md
  jurisdiction-uk.md
  …
skills/slo-legal/SKILL.md → cites _biz-shared/
```

- **Trade-offs**: keeps everything under `skills/`, which is the install unit. Matches how some ecosystems ship "foundation" skills. Risk: the leading-underscore convention isn't established in this repo and may confuse `sldo-install` (needs verification — would `./target/release/sldo-install` treat `_biz-shared` as installable? The install manifest contract from `~/.sldo/install.toml` might or might not).
- **Cost**: introduces a new repo convention. No precedent in this repo.
- **Best fit**: only if the biz pack grows beyond advisor skills and generators need to cite the same scaffolding — not obvious at v1.

### Option 3 — Per-skill self-contained (Approach A from the idea doc)

Structure:

```
skills/slo-legal/SKILL.md + inlined triage + inlined jurisdiction + inlined cost table
skills/slo-accounting/SKILL.md + inlined triage …
```

- **Trade-offs**: fastest to ship M1 (nothing to coordinate). But after 2-3 revisions the four advisor skills' triage gates **will** drift — the idea doc correctly identifies this as the predictable failure.
- **Best fit**: only if the pack stays at 1-2 advisor skills. Once four, Option 1 wins.

### Option 4 — Hybrid: Option 1 structure, but `/slo-legal` v1 ships `references/biz/` as *empty placeholder files* with link-only content

- Ship the v1 wedge with **minimum viable shared refs**: `triage-gate.md` and `cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md` only. The other shared files land in M2 with `/slo-accounting` when the second advisor skill exists to cite them.
- **Trade-offs**: avoids premature abstraction; respects "three similar lines is better than a premature abstraction" from the project's own CLAUDE.md preamble. Still gets the one-source-of-truth discipline for the triage gate (the most drift-prone surface).
- **Best fit**: a pragmatic compromise between Options 1 and 3; probably the highest-value path if M1 velocity matters more than complete scaffolding.
