# Synthesis — what this means for the design

## The price wedge collapses; the integration wedge holds

The idea-doc anchored its ROI on a £5,700 Russell Cooke baseline, implying a price advantage versus regulated solicitor work. Research dissolves that frame: the cheapest credible UK template substitute is **£17.49–£19.99/mo** (Rocket Lawyer UK Rocket Legal+ at £17.49/mo billed £209.88/yr; Sparqa Legal at £19.99 + VAT/mo), with SeedLegals Access at £75/mo bundling unlimited templates plus product support. None of these competitors integrate with the SLO engineering loop, none expose a machine-readable triage gate that other skills can cite, and none commit to a refusal posture for regulated/high-value/counterparty-with-lawyer/GDPR contexts. **The design must position `/slo-legal` v1 on workflow integration plus triage authority, not raw price, because Rocket Lawyer UK at £17.49/mo and Sparqa at £19.99/mo (sources: rocketlawyer.com/gb, sparqa.com) collapse any pure-cost narrative.**

## The "hard-block all GDPR drafts" rule is a decision, not a research gap

ICO enforcement evidence for sub-£1M-turnover private companies in the 2024-04 to 2026-04 window shows monetary penalties are **PECR-direct-marketing-dominated**, not Article-13-privacy-notice-dominated — examples cluster at AFK £90k, Skean Homes £100k, LADH £50k, Pinnacle Life £80k, Dr Telemarketing £100k, Poxell £150k, and standalone "bad privacy notice" enforcement against small private companies in this window is effectively zero (sources: BDO, URM Consulting H1 2025, Lewis Silkin 2024 round-up, Lexology). At the same time, DUAA 2025 Stage 3 (commenced 5 February 2026) raised the PECR ceiling to £17.5M / 4% global turnover, codified a new 7th lawful basis ("Recognised Legitimate Interests"), narrowed Article 22 to special-category data, and adds a complaints-procedure duty for all controllers from 19 June 2026 (sources: legislation.gov.uk DUAA 2025, ICO summary, Clifford Chance, Hogan Lovells). Two defensible postures exist: (a) keep the broad block on professional-negligence + upside-asymmetry grounds — one bad lawful-basis call can unwind a B2C launch and the £17.5M ceiling makes the tail unbounded; (b) narrow to direct-marketing/PECR contexts where enforcement actually clusters. **The design must surface this calibration decision to Sherif as an explicit GDPR-rule waiver in `/slo-architect`, before any triage prose is frozen, because the enforcement evidence (ICO public register Apr 2024 – Apr 2026) and the legal-baseline shift (DUAA 2025 Stage 3 commenced 5 Feb 2026) point in opposite directions on calibration.**

## US/EU is a v2 architectural pivot, not a config flag

No surveyed prior art uses a "shared-prose with jurisdiction flag" architecture. Stripe Atlas and Clerky stayed Delaware-only by design, with Cooley- and Orrick/Wilson-Sonsini-drafted templates respectively (sources: stripe.com/atlas, clerky.com/formation, Orrick Legal Guide PDF). SeedLegals runs **true parallel per-jurisdiction stacks** (UK, US, Ireland, France) at ~160 staff; the France build required **six months** to internalise local rules including the BSPCE share-option regime (source: Sifted — UK's SeedLegals launches in France). Anthony Rose (CEO, SeedLegals) explicit on the design choice: "different jurisdictions require different legal infrastructure, but the principles remain the same" — triage logic transfers; prose/clauses do not. US prose reuse for IP assignment is ~40–60% because commissioned software is generally NOT a 17 USC §101 work-made-for-hire-eligible category, and US T&Cs differ on consumer-rights regime (Magnuson-Moss vs CRA 2015), arbitration norms, and choice-of-law/venue. Templating "EU" as a single jurisdiction is a category error — the natural seam is `eu-<de|fr|es|nl|...>.md`. **The design must ship UK-only with a clear "UK only in v1" error surface — not a `--jurisdiction` flag that implies false portability — because every surveyed prior art (Stripe Atlas / Clerky / SeedLegals) chose either single-jurisdiction depth or parallel per-country modules over a shared-prose-with-flag pattern.**

## oneNDA's CC BY-ND license shapes how `/slo-legal draft nda` renders

oneNDA (TLB consortium, 2021; sources: onenda.org) is UK-drafted, lawyer-reviewed, and the only credible **open-standard NDA template** with a canonical England & Wales Country Schedule. Its **CC BY-ND 4.0** licence forbids derivative works, which means a `/slo-legal draft nda` that emits *modified* oneNDA prose may breach the licence. The safe pattern: render the oneNDA template **unmodified** and emit the company-specific fields, counterparty details, and cover page as a separately-rendered artefact that wraps but does not edit the canonical text. This both reduces liability and gives `/slo-legal` an auditable source-of-truth citation. No equivalent CC-licensed UK-native template surfaced for contractor SOW, IP assignment, or T&Cs (Kindrik Partners / Simmonds Stewart NZ-law templates give ~80% structural overlap but require E&W rewrite of governing-law and statutory references). **The design must split `draft nda` into two files — a verbatim oneNDA template body (unedited) plus a separately-rendered cover-and-fields page — and `/slo-verify` must contain a regression test that the rendered NDA body matches canonical oneNDA bytes, because CC BY-ND 4.0 forbids derivatives (source: onenda.org licence + CC BY-ND 4.0 deed).**

## Shared scaffolding belongs at `references/biz/`, not under `skills/`

The internal source-of-truth check is `crates/sldo-install/src/install.rs:44-71` (`discover_skills()`): it iterates `<skills_dir>/*`, requires `<name>/SKILL.md` to exist, and skips directories whose names start with `.` — but **does not** filter leading underscore. A `skills/_biz-shared/` directory would only be ignored by the installer if it lacked a `SKILL.md`, leaving an undocumented convention (Option B of the dossier's architecture options) that breaks the moment someone adds `SKILL.md`. Putting shared refs at **`references/biz/`** at the repo root has zero installer interaction, mirrors the existing per-skill `references/` precedent at the package level, and is the dossier's recommended Option A. **The design must place shared advisor-skill scaffolding at `references/biz/` outside the `skills/` tree, because `crates/sldo-install/src/install.rs:44-71` confirms the installer will silently include any new `skills/<name>/` that has a `SKILL.md`, making an `_biz-shared/` convention fragile by default.**

## M1 footprint: hybrid Option D — ship two shared refs, defer the rest

CLAUDE.md's preamble ("three similar lines is better than a premature abstraction") plus the dossier's Option D recommendation converge: ship M1 with only two shared reference files — `references/biz/triage-gate.md` (the four hard-blocks: regulated / >£5,000 / counterparty-with-lawyer / GDPR-doc) and `references/biz/cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md` (with explicit provenance line and retrieval date) — and defer `jurisdiction-uk.md`, `jurisdiction-us.md`, `jurisdiction-eu.md`, `artifact-schema.md`, `hmrc-vcm-index.md`, `ico-duaa-index.md`, `ir35-cest-factors.md`, `ico-enforcement-reality.md`, `open-template-anchors.md` until M2 (`/slo-accounting`) when a second consumer exists. **The design must establish the shared-refs pattern in M1 with exactly two files (not the full Option A skeleton) because the four-skill advisor cluster needs one source-of-truth for the drift-prone triage gate first, and the rest of the scaffolding has no second consumer until M2 (per Option D in the dossier and CLAUDE.md's anti-premature-abstraction rule).**

## SEIS/EIS, IR35, and employment triage have authoritative anchors — no fresh research needed for triage prose

Q3 (SEIS/EIS), Q4 (employment + IR35), and parts of Q5 (US WMFH doctrine) all surfaced authoritative HMRC + legislation.gov.uk + commentary anchors sufficient for triage-gate wording in M3 (`/slo-equity`) and M4 (`/slo-fundraise`) of Runbook A, and for `/slo-hire` in Runbook C. Specifically: VCM34080 (control / disqualifying arrangements), VCM3000 (excluded activities), VCM31000 (SEIS income tax relief); Advance Assurance lead time 4–6 weeks realistic with apply-≥6-weeks-before-term-sheet floor; ERA 1996 s86 notice schedule, WTR 1998 5.6 weeks holiday, Pensions Act 2008 thresholds 2026/27 (£10,000 trigger); CEST April 2025 refresh with ~20% indeterminate rate and documented MOO blind spot per HMRC's reading of *PGMOL v HMRC* [2024] UKSC 29 (sources: gov.uk VCM manual, gov.uk CEST page, Bird & Bird "Spot the Difference" April 2025, ContractorUK 2026/27 explainer). **The design must wire these gov.uk URLs as cited reference anchors in `/slo-fundraise`, `/slo-equity`, and `/slo-hire` triage prose — with retrieval-date stamps, not copy-pasted figures — because the underlying rates and thresholds (SSP £116.75/wk 2024-25 baseline; pension thresholds £10,000/£6,240/£50,270 held flat for 2026/27; IR35 small-company turnover threshold £10.2M → £15M effective 6 April 2026) all uprate annually and copy-pasting bakes in stale figures (sources: GOV.UK pension-thresholds 2026/27 page, Greenberg Traurig March 2026 IR35 threshold-change article).**
