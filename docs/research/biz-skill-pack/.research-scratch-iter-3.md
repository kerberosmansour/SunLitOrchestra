## Deepened Findings

### R1 — `sldo-install` treatment of underscore-prefixed directories (sourced, high confidence)

From `crates/sldo-install/src/install.rs:44-71`, `discover_skills()`:
- Iterates `<skills_dir>/*`, requires `<name>/SKILL.md` to exist, skips names starting with `.`.
- **Leading underscore is NOT filtered.** A `skills/_biz-shared/` directory will be discovered if and only if it contains a `SKILL.md`.
- Implication: a shared-scaffold directory CAN live under `skills/` without being installed, but only if it **lacks** `SKILL.md`. That makes `skills/_biz-shared/` a silent-no-op from the installer's perspective — acceptable but convention-novel in this repo (no precedent). Putting shared refs under `references/biz/` at repo root (outside `skills/`) has zero installer interaction and matches the existing `skills/slo-critique/references/` per-skill pattern.

### R2 — DUAA 2025 specific obligations (high confidence)

Stage 3 commenced **5 February 2026** (Commencement No. 6 Regs) and is what `/slo-legal` must assume:
- **New 7th lawful basis** — "Recognised Legitimate Interests" (Sch. 4 UK GDPR Annex 1): national security, emergencies, safeguarding, crime detection, disclosures to public bodies. No LIA balancing required, but must still be named in the privacy notice.
- **Legitimate-interest examples codified**: direct marketing, intra-group admin, network security.
- **Article 22 narrowed**: solely-automated decisions trigger protection only when involving special category data. Material relief for non-SCD automated scoring.
- **DSAR proportionality + "stop the clock"** on ID verification codified.
- **PECR fine ceiling raised to £17.5M / 4% global turnover** (from £500k) — direct marketing and cookies are now UK-GDPR-scale risk.
- **Complaints procedure duty**: all controllers must publish one by **19 June 2026** regardless of size.

No small-company exemption created. (Sources: [legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/18](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/18), [ICO DUAA summary](https://ico.org.uk/about-the-ico/what-we-do/legislation-we-cover/data-use-and-access-act-2025/the-data-use-and-access-act-2025-duaa-summary-of-the-changes/), [Clifford Chance Feb 2026](https://www.cliffordchance.com/insights/resources/blogs/talking-tech/en/articles/2026/02/key-aspects-of-the-data--use-and-access--act-take-effect.html), [Hogan Lovells](https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/uks-data-use-and-access-act-2025-data-protection-provisions-come-into-force).)

### R3 — ICO enforcement reality against sub-seed startups (high confidence, contradicts prior round)

**This is the biggest research update vs iteration 2.** Register analysis Apr 2024 – Apr 2026:
- Monetary penalties on sub-£1M-turnover private companies are **concentrated in PECR direct-marketing breaches** (AFK £90k, Skean Homes £100k, LADH £50k, Pinnacle Life £80k, Dr Telemarketing £100k, Poxell £150k) — all nuisance-call operators.
- **Standalone "bad privacy notice / Article 13 transparency" enforcement against small private companies is effectively zero in the 24-month window.**
- Reprimands went overwhelmingly to public-sector bodies (NHS trusts, councils, police, Labour Party). ([URM Jan–Jun 2025 analysis](https://www.urmconsulting.com/blog/analysis-of-ico-enforcement-action-january-june-2025), [Lewis Silkin 2024 round-up](https://www.lewissilkin.com/en/insights/2024/05/30/the-ico-fines-again-a-round-up-of-fines-issued-by-the-ico-in-2024))

**Implication** (inference, not sourced): the previous round's "hard-block draft for ALL GDPR documents" posture is **defensible on professional-negligence and upside-asymmetry grounds**, but NOT defensible on pure enforcement-risk grounds. A more honest framing for the triage gate: translate/triage-only for privacy notice / ROPA / DPA, but the *enforcement-quantified* hard-block should be **PECR/direct-marketing-specific** — that's where small companies actually get fined.

### R4 — CEST April 2025 refresh (high confidence)

HMRC released a CEST refresh **30 April 2025**: questionnaire restructured into ~6 sections, language simplified, MOO section added, new gating question requires a contract to exist. Substitution tightened — **the right must be unrestricted and genuinely exercisable** (merely recommending a replacement no longer counts). HMRC explicit: "no change to the underlying technical principles" — the engine is unchanged.

Key limitations that the `/slo-legal` / `/slo-hire` triage gate must surface:
- CEST still **assumes MOO exists** in any engagement that reaches the questionnaire (HMRC reading of *PGMOL v HMRC* [2024] UKSC 29).
- A CEST "outside IR35" result is **not legally binding** on HMRC — only "stood by" if inputs match reality.

(Sources: [Bird & Bird "Spot the Difference"](https://www.twobirds.com/en/insights/2025/uk/spot-the-difference-a-closer-look-at-hmrcs-updated-cest-tool), [Pinsent Masons CEST 2.0](https://www.pinsentmasons.com/en-gb/out-law/news/hmrc-releases-cest-20ir35-status-determination-tool), [gov.uk CEST](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/check-employment-status-for-tax), [Kingsbridge April 2025 review](https://www.kingsbridge.co.uk/blog/contractors/ir35/hmrc-cest-update-april-2025-review/).)

### R5 — SEIS/EIS Advance Assurance lead time (high confidence)

- HMRC internal target ~15 working days; realistic 4–6 weeks; ~26% of applications exceeded 30 days in tax year 2022-23.
- SeedLegals-prepared applications typically clear in 2–3 weeks after their 1–2 week pre-submission review. End-to-end planning figure: **3–5 weeks, 6+ if HMRC follow-up**.
- Carta UK + Undo Capital both advise applying **1–2 months before** approaching investors. Practical floor: **6 weeks before term-sheet signature**.
- SeedLegals 2026: AA is bundled into **Plus plan £999/yr +VAT** (not sold standalone). £500 add-on if no prior AA / round off-platform / nominee used.
- Legally optional; practically required (UK angels routinely gate wire on sight of AA).

(Sources: [gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-advance-assurance](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/venture-capital-schemes-apply-for-advance-assurance), [SeedLegals "How long do HMRC take"](https://help.seedlegals.com/en/2137054-how-long-do-hmrc-take-to-approve-seis-eis-applications), [Jonathan Lea Network](https://www.jonathanlea.net/blog/how-long-does-it-take-to-get-seis-and-eis-advance-assurance/), [Carta UK](https://carta.com/uk/en/learn/startups/fundraising/seis-eis-advance-assurance/).)

### R8 — Open-standard UK templates (high confidence, material to v1)

- **oneNDA** is UK-drafted (TLB, 2021), CC BY-ND 4.0, uses a **Country Schedule** — core template is jurisdiction-neutral with an England & Wales schedule as canonical. Directly usable for UK startups without modification beyond completing the cover page. ([onenda.org](https://onenda.org))
- **oneSaaS** (SaaS T&Cs, UK-drafted, 2023) and **oneDPA** (GDPR-aware DPA, 2024) exist from the same consortium.
- **No CC-licensed UK-native template exists** for contractor SOW or IP assignment. Nearest adaptations: Simmonds Stewart / Kindrik Partners (NZ law — light reskin needed; ~80% structural overlap but governing-law and statutory references must be swapped).

Implication: `/slo-legal` v1 can cite **oneNDA as canonical NDA source** (a liability-reducing anchor — "we render oneNDA, which has been lawyer-reviewed") but must draft contractor SOW, IP assignment, and T&Cs from scratch or adapt Kindrik/Simmonds Stewart with explicit E&W overlay.

## Architecture Options

All assume existing skill pattern (`skills/<name>/SKILL.md` + optional `references/` + optional `personas/` — precedent at `skills/slo-critique/`, `skills/slo-plan/`).

### Option A — `references/biz/` at repo root (carried forward, **now the primary recommendation**)

```
skills/slo-legal/SKILL.md
skills/slo-legal/templates/{nda.md,contractor-sow.md,ip-assignment.md,terms-and-conditions.md}
skills/slo-accounting/SKILL.md
skills/slo-equity/SKILL.md
skills/slo-fundraise/SKILL.md
references/biz/
  triage-gate.md                    # single source of truth for 4 gates
  jurisdiction-uk.md                # UK-only prose/anchors
  jurisdiction-us.md                # stub, returns "not supported" error surface
  jurisdiction-eu.md                # stub
  cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md  # with explicit provenance line
  artifact-schema.md                # docs/biz/ frontmatter contract
  hmrc-vcm-index.md                 # VCM34080, VCM3000 anchor list + date
  ico-duaa-index.md                 # DUAA commencement tracker + Feb 2026 anchor
  ir35-cest-factors.md              # MOO limitation + PGMOL note + 3-factor list
  ico-enforcement-reality.md        # PECR-not-Article-13 small-co pattern
  open-template-anchors.md          # oneNDA + Kindrik/Simmonds Stewart notes
```

Trade-offs: lives **outside** `skills/` so installer never sees it (confirmed by `install.rs:44`). Matches repo's `skills/<name>/references/` convention at the package level. Forces advisor skills to cite one file per gate (discipline, not enforcement).

Best fit: the four-advisor UK-first pack as currently scoped. **Primary recommendation.**

### Option B — `skills/_biz-shared/` as a sibling skill without `SKILL.md`

Because `install.rs` skips directories without `SKILL.md`, `skills/_biz-shared/` would be silently ignored by the installer. Content lives co-located with the skills that cite it.

Trade-offs: novel convention in this repo; leading-underscore convention is not documented or tested. `sldo-install --dry-run` would give no signal that `_biz-shared/` is meant to be shared (it just quietly disappears). Someone adding a `SKILL.md` later would accidentally publish it. **Option A avoids all of this for free** by putting shared refs outside `skills/`.

Best fit: only if the biz pack later needs shared *executable skills* (not just refs). Not justified at v1.

### Option C — Per-skill inlined (Approach A from idea doc)

`skills/slo-legal/SKILL.md` inlines triage + jurisdiction + cost table. Each advisor skill owns its copy.

Trade-offs: fastest M1 ship. But with four advisor skills sharing the same triage criteria (regulated matter / deal value / counterparty lawyer / GDPR), drift is the **predictable** failure mode — and the previous round's GDPR hard-block rule is exactly the kind of rule that would drift inconsistently across the four skills. The new R3 finding (that PECR, not Article 13, is the actual enforcement pattern) would have to be patched in four places.

Best fit: only if pack stays at 1–2 advisor skills.

### Option D — Hybrid: Option A skeleton but M1 ships only 2 shared refs

Ship M1 with only `references/biz/triage-gate.md` and `references/biz/cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md` under the shared root. Other shared files land in M2 when `/slo-accounting` exists to cite them.

Trade-offs: respects project CLAUDE.md preamble ("three similar lines is better than a premature abstraction"). Still establishes the one-source-of-truth discipline for the drift-prone surface (the triage gate). Defers schema-design work on the jurisdiction matrix and HMRC/ICO indexes until a second consumer exists.

Best fit: highest-value path if M1 velocity matters more than complete scaffolding. **Recommended if the runbook budget for M1 is tight (≤1 week).**

## Unanswered Questions

1. **Is the "hard-block draft for ALL GDPR documents" posture the right calibration?** R3 changed the evidence base — ICO enforcement against small private companies is PECR-dominated, not Article-13-dominated. A narrower rule (translate/triage only for privacy notice; hard-block only for consumer-marketing/PECR contexts) is now defensible. Needs an explicit founder + Sherif decision before `/slo-legal` triage wording is frozen.

2. **Russell Cooke 2026-27 price list provenance.** R7 confirms it is not publicly retrievable. Sherif should either (a) provide the private PDF + retrieval date for `references/biz/cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md`, or (b) swap to a publicly-itemised baseline (JPP Law) so the ROI claim is auditable.

3. **oneNDA ND license implications for `draft` mode.** CC BY-ND 4.0 forbids derivative works. A `/slo-legal draft nda` that emits a modified oneNDA text may breach the license; the safe pattern is render-the-unmodified-template + separately-generated cover page / schedule. Needs an explicit SKILL.md rule, and possibly a test in `slo-verify`.

4. **Is there an equivalent to oneNDA for UK contractor SOW / IP assignment / T&Cs as of 2026?** R8 found no CC-licensed UK-native template for these three. Worth one targeted search against Practical Law free precedents, the IPO model clauses, and any Law Society / techUK model templates before drafting from scratch.

5. **Post-DUAA complaints-procedure duty commencement (19 Jun 2026).** `/slo-legal` GDPR triage needs to emit a warning if run between 2026-06-19 onward and the founder has no published complaints procedure. Needs wording.

6. **Small-company IR35 threshold change 6 April 2026** (£10.2M → £15M turnover, £5.1M → £7.5M balance sheet). Seed-stage founders remain inside "small" post-threshold; contractor-side self-assessment continues. Needs an explicit line in `ir35-cest-factors.md`.

7. **Cross-check whether `--jurisdiction us` / `--jurisdiction eu` stubs are more honest than no flag at all.** R5 (prior round) + R8 (this round) both say US/EU is a new build per country, not a config flip. An error surface that says "UK only in v1" is truthful; a `--jurisdiction` flag implies false portability. Defer until v2 architectural pivot.

8. **Open standards for shareholders agreement / EMI option scheme.** Out of scope for `/slo-legal` v1 but needed by `/slo-equity` and `/slo-fundraise`. oneNDA consortium has not published these; SeedLegals templates are proprietary. Needs a dedicated research slice before Runbook A M3.
