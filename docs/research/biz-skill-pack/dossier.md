# Research Dossier — Business-side skill pack for SLO

## Market

UK seed-stage founders pay for the company-around-the-product side of a startup through three substitute channels: (1) per-document instructions to a regulated solicitor at fixed fees (Russell Cooke 2026-27 baseline: ~£5,700 for an NDA + Contractor SOW + IP Assignment + T&Cs bundle); (2) DIY template subscriptions at £17–£35/mo (Rocket Lawyer UK, Sparqa Legal, Genie AI, SeedLegals Access at £75/mo for the full membership); (3) a regulated end-to-end SaaS like SeedLegals Funding at £1,490–£4,990/yr that bundles round tooling and an account-managed advance-assurance service. The cheapest credible substitute for a UK template bundle is **£17.49–£19.99/mo**, not the £5,700 anchor in the idea doc — so `/slo-legal` v1 cannot win on price. The defensible position is *workflow integration inside the SLO loop + triage authority*, with the £5,700 figure reframed as opportunity-cost provenance for ROI claims rather than a price wedge.

## Technical prior art

- **oneNDA** (TLB consortium, 2021) — UK-drafted open-standard NDA, **CC BY-ND 4.0** licensed, jurisdiction-neutral core with an England & Wales Country Schedule. The ND clause forbids derivative works; `/slo-legal draft nda` must render the unmodified template + emit fields/cover-page separately, not edit the prose. https://www.onenda.org/
- **oneSaaS** (UK SaaS T&Cs, 2023) and **oneDPA** (GDPR DPA, 2024) — same consortium; candidate anchors for `/slo-legal` M3+ T&Cs and GDPR DPA. Not needed for M1.
- **`ai-legal-claude`** (GitHub `zubair-trabzada/ai-legal-claude`) — open-source Claude Code skill for contract review/NDA generation. Confirms the in-Claude legal-skill packaging model is viable; we differ on UK-jurisdiction discipline, advisor-mode triage, and SLO loop integration.
- **Docassemble** (MIT) — open-source legal-doc automation; security checklist refreshed 2025-12. Inspiration for `draft`-mode question-tree design; not a runtime dependency.
- **Kindrik Partners / Simmonds Stewart** — CC-licensed NZ-law startup template library; structural reference for contractor SOW + IP assignment, requires E&W rewrite of governing-law and statutory references.
- **HMRC Venture Capital Schemes Manual** (VCM34080 control/independence; VCM3000 excluded activities; VCM31000 SEIS income tax relief) — Crown copyright, OGL-reusable. Authoritative reference for `/slo-fundraise` triage gate.
- **HMRC CEST tool** (April 2025 refresh, effective 30 April 2025) — gov.uk-hosted IR35 status-determination tool; ~20% indeterminate rate; documented MOO blind spot per HMRC's reading of *PGMOL v HMRC* [2024] UKSC 29; substitution criterion tightened to "unrestricted and genuinely exercisable." Authoritative for `/slo-hire` IR35 triage but explicitly insufficient on its own (Bird & Bird "Spot the Difference" April 2025 review).
- **legislation.gov.uk** — ERA 1996 s86 (notice), WTR 1998 (5.6 weeks holiday), Pensions Act 2008 + 2026/27 thresholds (£10,000 trigger / £6,240 lower / £50,270 upper qualifying earnings, held flat vs 2025/26), DUAA 2025 (Royal Assent 19 June 2025; Stage 3 commenced 5 February 2026; complaints-procedure duty from 19 June 2026; PECR ceiling £17.5M / 4% global turnover; new 7th lawful basis "Recognised Legitimate Interests"; Article 22 narrowed to special-category data).
- **`crates/sldo-install/src/install.rs:44-71`** (`discover_skills()`) — internal source-of-truth: iterates `<skills_dir>/*`, requires `<name>/SKILL.md`, skips `.`-prefixed names; **does not** filter leading underscore. Implication: shared scaffolding belongs at `references/biz/` outside `skills/` to guarantee zero installer interaction.
- **`python-docx` 1.2.0 (2025-06-16, MIT)** and **`docxtpl` 0.20.2 (2025-11-13, LGPL-3.0)** — surveyed and rejected for v1: Markdown-in-repo is the SLO convention, DOCX export is not on the critical path, and LGPL would taint the Rust workspace.

## Regulatory / legal

- **DUAA 2025 Stage 3 commenced 5 February 2026** — moving baseline that `/slo-legal` GDPR triage prose must reference: PECR fine ceiling raised £500k → £17.5M / 4% global turnover; new 7th lawful basis ("Recognised Legitimate Interests") with examples codified for direct marketing, intra-group admin, network security; Article 22 narrowed to special-category-data automated decisions; complaints-procedure duty for all controllers from **19 June 2026**.
- **ICO enforcement reality, sub-£1M private-company segment Apr 2024 – Apr 2026** — monetary penalties are **PECR-direct-marketing-dominated**, not Article-13-privacy-notice-dominated. Examples: AFK £90k, Skean Homes £100k, LADH £50k, Pinnacle Life £80k, Dr Telemarketing £100k, Poxell £150k. Standalone "bad privacy notice / Article 13" enforcement against small private companies in this window is **effectively zero**. Implication: the proposed "hard-block ALL GDPR drafts" rule is defensible on professional-negligence + upside-asymmetry grounds (one bad lawful-basis call can unwind a B2C launch) but **conservative** versus the enforcement evidence — a narrower posture (translate/triage for privacy notices; hard-block for direct-marketing/PECR) is also defensible. Risks #1.
- **oneNDA license — CC BY-ND 4.0** — forbids derivative works. `/slo-legal draft nda` that emits *modified* oneNDA prose may breach the licence. Safe pattern: render unmodified template + separately-generated cover page / schedule. Needs an explicit SKILL.md rule and a `/slo-verify` test that the rendered NDA prose body matches the canonical oneNDA bytes.
- **SEIS/EIS retroactive disqualification triggers** — five anchors `/slo-fundraise` triage must surface: (1) **breach of control / independence** (51% subsidiary or under control of another company / connected persons, HMRC VCM34080); (2) **disqualifying arrangements** (ITA07 s257HJ(1), VCM34080); (3) **preferential rights on share class** — even articles/SHA drafting that grants preferential rights without reissue; *Abingdon Health Ltd v HMRC* [2016] TC 05525, *Flix Innovations* line; (4) **qualifying-trade drift into excluded activities** (VCM3000); (5) **value extraction / non-independent transactions** post-raise. Practical floor: apply for Advance Assurance ≥ 6 weeks before term-sheet signature (HMRC internal target ~15 working days; realistic 4–6 weeks; ~26% of applications exceeded 30 days in 2022-23).
- **UK employment-law minimums for first hire** — ERA 1996 s86 notice (employer 1 wk if <2 yrs, +1 wk per completed year up to a max of 12 weeks at 12+ yrs; employee 1 wk after 1 month service); WTR 1998 5.6 weeks paid leave (28 days FT); SSP £116.75/week 2024-25 baseline; pension auto-enrolment trigger £10,000/yr (2026/27, held flat); right-to-work checks under IANA 2006 s15 (IDVT permitted for British/Irish passport holders); NI/PAYE registration before first paycheque.
- **IR35 / CEST limitations** — three indicators: substitution (single strongest outside-IR35 factor; April 2025 refresh requires it be "unrestricted and genuinely exercisable"), MOO (CEST does **not** weigh this — HMRC reads *PGMOL v HMRC* [2024] UKSC 29 as MOO existing in any contract for services), and control. CEST returns "unable to determine" in ~20% of cases (34 of 72 outcome routes). CEST is necessary but not sufficient — Bird & Bird, ContractorUK, ir35guide all warn against relying on CEST alone. **Hard-block-to-lawyer surface:** absent or sham substitution clause; contractor substantially full-time, exclusive, under direction; CEST "employed" or "unable to determine" result; engagement >6 months with rolling renewals; contractor uses engager equipment + attends engager premises. Small-company IR35 thresholds change 6 April 2026 (turnover £10.2M → £15M; balance sheet £5.1M → £7.5M; headcount 50 unchanged) — seed-stage clients remain "small" and continue to rely on contractor self-assessment, not engager-side determination.
- **Jurisdiction expansion** — no surveyed prior art uses a "shared-prose + jurisdiction-flag" architecture. The market is converged on either single-jurisdiction depth (Stripe Atlas, Clerky — both Delaware-only by design) or parallel per-country modules (SeedLegals; the France build took **six months** to internalise local rules including BSPCE share-option regime). Anthony Rose (SeedLegals CEO) explicit: "different jurisdictions require different legal infrastructure, but the principles remain the same" — triage logic transfers; prose/clauses do not. US prose reuse for IP assignment ~40–60% (governing-law + WMFH-recital paragraph differ; commissioned software is generally NOT a 17 USC §101 work-made-for-hire-eligible category); EU "single jurisdiction" is a category error — natural seam is `eu-<de|fr|es|nl|...>.md`.

## Open questions that research did not answer

- **GDPR-rule calibration is a decision, not a research gap.** Two defensible positions exist (broad block on all GDPR drafts vs narrower block scoped to direct-marketing/PECR contexts where ICO enforcement actually clusters). Needs an explicit founder + Sherif decision before `/slo-legal` triage wording is frozen — not more research. **Highest pre-ship blocker.**
- **Russell Cooke 2026-27 price list provenance.** The list is not publicly retrievable — no PDF or page on russell-cooke.co.uk matches. Needs either (a) the private PDF + retrieval date for `references/biz/cost-baseline-russell-cooke-2026.md`, or (b) swap to a publicly-itemised baseline like JPP Law fixed-fee startup pricing so the ROI claim is auditable.
- **Equivalent of oneNDA for UK contractor SOW / IP assignment / T&Cs as of 2026?** No CC-licensed UK-native template surfaced. Worth one targeted search against Practical Law free precedents, IPO model clauses, and Law Society / techUK model templates before drafting from scratch — research did not exhaust these specific publishers.
- **Article-13 / lawful-basis enforcement against private companies under £1M turnover or under 50 employees: granular split.** ICO does not publish a size-segmented register; the Apify UK-ICO-Enforcement-Actions dataset has no turnover/headcount fields. The "effectively zero" finding is best-effort manual analysis of the public register, not a published statistic.
- **Open-standard shareholders agreement / EMI option scheme.** Out of scope for `/slo-legal` v1 but needed by `/slo-equity` and `/slo-fundraise`. oneNDA consortium has not published these; SeedLegals templates are proprietary. Needs a dedicated research slice before Runbook A M3.
- **CEST April 2025 refresh effect on IR35 triage prose.** Substitution is now stricter; needs one re-read of the Bird & Bird "Spot the Difference" analysis against the current CEST UI before `/slo-hire` and `/slo-legal` IR35 triage prose are frozen for M1.
